What makes a “good” book?

Analyzing Goodreads Users

Popularity vs. Engagement

This section explores what really defines a “good” book. Is it the number of people who read it? The volume of reviews and discussion? Or the average rating it receives? As you look through this visualization, you’ll be able to compare popularity, engagement, and high ratings side by side to see how each one shapes the way readers perceive quality.

We’ll also dig into whether Goodreads users tend to rate generously, or whether some books spark more critical responses than others. And as you explore, consider a bigger question: Are widely loved books truly better, or are niche favorites overlooked gems?

Popularity vs. Engagement

This visualization explores how a book’s popularity (total ratings) connects with its engagement (total reviews). A steady upward trend signals that as books receive more reviews, they also tend to accumulate more ratings. At first glance, this might suggest a simple pattern—more conversation equals more visibility, and more visibility means more people rating the book. But then there are the exceptions—the runaway hits.

But is it really that straightforward? Maybe high engagement reflects passionate fan communities. Or perhaps strong marketing pushes create book-to-film moments that dominate cultural timelines. On the other hand, could it mean that viewers only feel compelled to write reviews when a book is especially love-it or hate-it?
